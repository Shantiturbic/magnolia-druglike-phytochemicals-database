# Magnolia Taxonomy, Phylogeny, and Phytochemistry Research

Compiled: 2026-05-21 (updated with additional searches same date)
Purpose: Support taxonomic and phytochemical justification for BBB database construction from Dominican Republic Magnolia species.

---

## 1. Accepted Genera in Magnoliaceae (APG IV)

### Current Classification

Under the APG IV system, the family Magnoliaceae is placed in order Magnoliales within the magnoliid clade. The family comprises **two subfamilies**:

1. **Magnolioideae** -- contains a single genus: **Magnolia** L.
2. **Liriodendroideae** -- contains a single genus: **Liriodendron** L. (2 species: *L. tulipifera* and *L. chinense*)

Thus, under the broad (sensu lato) treatment now widely adopted, **Magnoliaceae contains only 2 accepted genera**.

### Total Species

Estimates vary by source and year:
- **~300 species** in the family (widely cited figure; Wikipedia, GBIF)
- **~350--365 species** of Magnolia s.l. according to the World Flora Online Database
- **~223 species** in more conservative treatments (Flora of the Southeastern United States, 2025 edition)
- Liriodendron adds only 2 species

The variation reflects ongoing species descriptions (especially Neotropical taxa) and differing species concepts.

### Genera Merged into Magnolia sensu lato by Figlar & Nooteboom (2004)

Figlar and Nooteboom published "Notes on Magnoliaceae IV" in *Blumea -- Biodiversity, Evolution and Biogeography of Plants* 49: 87--100 (2004). Based on molecular (DNA) and morphological evidence, they erected a new classification for subfamily Magnolioideae consisting of a **single genus Magnolia**, with the former genera demoted to sections and subsections. The genera merged include:

| Former Genus | Geographic Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Talauma** Juss. | Neotropics, SE Asia | Now Magnolia sect. Talauma |
| **Michelia** L. | Asia | Now Magnolia sect. Michelia |
| **Manglietia** Blume | Asia | Now Magnolia sect. Manglietia |
| **Elmerrillia** Dandy | SE Asia (Philippines, Borneo) | Merged into Michelia/Magnolia |
| **Kmeria** (Pierre) Dandy | SE Asia (Vietnam, Cambodia) | Now section within Magnolia |
| **Pachylarnax** Dandy | Asia (India, Myanmar) | Merged into Manglietia/Magnolia |
| **Parakmeria** Hu & W.C.Cheng | China | Now section within Magnolia |
| **Tsoongiodendron** Chun | China | Merged into Magnolia |
| **Alcimandra** Dandy | Asia (India) | Merged into Magnolia |
| **Aromadendron** Blume | SE Asia (Malesia) | Merged into Magnolia |
| **Paramichelia** Hu | Asia | Merged into Michelia/Magnolia |

Prior to 2004, up to 17 genera had been recognized within Magnolioideae. Molecular phylogenetics showed that Manglietia, Michelia, and the remaining minor genera were nested among (not sister to) the various sections of Magnolia, making the multi-genus system paraphyletic.

The 2004 system recognizes **3 subgenera and 12 sections** within Magnolia s.l., with the 13 or so constituent clades accounted for at lower ranks. Wang et al. (2020) later refined this to **15 major clades** based on whole plastid genome sequences.

## 2. Magnolia Species Endemic to the Dominican Republic and Hispaniola

### Species List

Five endemic Magnolia species occur on the island of Hispaniola, all belonging to Magnolia sect. Talauma, subsect. Cubenses (= subsect. Splendentes sensu Figlar & Nooteboom 2004):

| Species | Common Name | Distribution | IUCN Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Magnolia pallescens** Urb. & Ekman | Ebano verde | Dominican Republic (Cordillera Central) | **Endangered (EN)** |
| **Magnolia domingensis** Urb. | Guaconejo | Dominican Republic + Haiti | **Critically Endangered (CR)** |
| **Magnolia hamorii** Howard | Tabacon | Dominican Republic (Barahona, Sierra de Neiba) | **Endangered (EN)** |
| **Magnolia ekmanii** Urb. | Ekman's magnolia | Haiti (Massif de la Hotte) | **Critically Endangered (CR)** |
| **Magnolia emarginata** Urb. & Ekman | Northern Haiti magnolia | Haiti (northern mountains) | **Critically Endangered (CR)** |

### Synonyms (former Talauma names)

- *Magnolia pallescens* = *Talauma pallescens* (Urb. & Ekman) A.Lozano
- *Magnolia domingensis* = *Talauma domingensis* Urb.
- *Magnolia hamorii* = *Talauma hamorii* Howard

### Conservation Context

All five species are restricted to wet montane cloud forests above ~700 m elevation. Threats include:
- Indiscriminate felling for cabinet wood (especially *M. pallescens* = ebano verde)
- Slash-and-burn agriculture
- Forest fires
- Charcoal production (Haiti)
- Conversion to grazing land

The Ebano Verde Scientific Reserve (23 km^2) was created in 1989 to protect *M. pallescens*. BGCI published an Integrated Conservation Action Plan for the three Dominican species (Magnolia domingensis -- M. hamorii -- M. pallescens) in 2023. *M. emarginata* was rediscovered in 2022 after being lost to science since 1925.

### Population Genetics

Claerhout et al. (2023) conducted an integrative study using species distribution modelling (SDM) with 21 environmental variables on 635 occurrences, plus genotyping of 417 individuals using 16 microsatellite markers. The three Dominican Magnolias were structured into five populations showing ample genetic diversity and little inbreeding overall.

**Full citation:** Claerhout T, Strubbe D, Castillo Torres R, Samain M-S, Chatrou L, Veltjen E. 2023. "An integrative study of species distribution modelling and conservation genetics: Magnolia in Hispaniola." *Biodiversity and Conservation* 32(4): 1205--1231. DOI: 10.1007/s10531-023-02546-6

### Published Phytochemistry Studies on Dominican Republic Magnolia Species

**No phytochemistry studies specific to Dominican Republic endemic Magnolia species were found.** The extensive phytochemical literature on the genus Magnolia is overwhelmingly concentrated on Asian species (especially *M. officinalis*, *M. obovata*, *M. grandiflora*, *M. biondii*). No published reports of secondary metabolite isolation, chemical profiling, or bioactivity screening were identified for *M. pallescens*, *M. domingensis*, or *M. hamorii* in any of the major review articles (Huyen et al. 2025; Lee et al. 2011; Poivre & Duez 2017) or primary literature databases.

This represents a **critical knowledge gap** and a key justification for the BBB database approach: chemotaxonomic inference from well-studied congeners is necessary because direct phytochemical data do not exist for Dominican Magnolia species.

## 3. Neotropical and Caribbean Magnolia Species

### Overview

Approximately **100 out of 108** New World Magnolia species are Neotropical (distributed from Tropic of Cancer to Tropic of Capricorn), with significant diversity in Mexico, Central America, and northern South America. This makes the Neotropics one of the two major diversity centers for the genus (the other being East and Southeast Asia).

### Caribbean Species (15 taxa: 12 species + 3 subspecies)

All Caribbean magnolias belong to **Magnolia sect. Talauma** (except *M. virginiana* subsp. *oviedoae*, placed in sect. Magnolia). A new section, **Magnolia sect. Splendentes**, was proposed for the Caribbean clade, though the nomenclaturally correct name is **subsect. Cubenses**.

| Species | Island(s) | IUCN Status |
|---|---|---|
| *M. cubensis* subsp. *cubensis* | Cuba (Sierra Maestra) | CR |
| *M. cubensis* subsp. *acunae* | Cuba (Guamuhaya Mtns.) | CR |
| *M. cristalensis* | Cuba (Nipe-Sagua-Baracoa) | CR |
| *M. minor* | Cuba | CR |
| *M. orbiculata* | Cuba | CR |
| *M. cacuminicola* | Cuba | CR |
| *M. oblongifolia* | Cuba | CR |
| *M. domingensis* | Hispaniola (DR + Haiti) | CR |
| *M. ekmanii* | Hispaniola (Haiti) | CR |
| *M. emarginata* | Hispaniola (Haiti) | CR |
| *M. hamorii* | Hispaniola (DR) | EN |
| *M. pallescens* | Hispaniola (DR) | EN |

Magnolia arrived in the Caribbean in **four independent colonization events** according to Veltjen et al. (2022). When multiple species co-occur on one island, they typically occupy different mountain chains separated by 30--400 km.

### Neotropical Mainland Species (selected)

Key Neotropical mainland species include taxa in Mexico, Central America, and South America:
- Mexico: *M. schiedeana*, *M. dealbata*, *M. mexicana*, *M. pugana*, *M. nuevoleonensis*, *M. zoquepopolucae*
- Central America: *M. yoroconte* (Honduras), *M. guatemalensis*, *M. poasana* (Costa Rica)
- South America: *M. sambuensis* (Colombia), *M. calimaensis* (Colombia), *M. vargasiana* (Ecuador)

### Phytochemistry of Caribbean / Neotropical Magnolia Species

**Limited but more informative than previously recognized.** While no Caribbean endemic Magnolia has been phytochemically profiled, several mainland Neotropical Talauma species have been studied — providing critical intermediate data points for chemotaxonomic inference.

#### Mainland Neotropical Talauma (sect. Talauma — same section as DR Cubenses)

- **Talauma ovata (= Magnolia ovata)** (Brazil, sect. Talauma): The most-studied Neotropical Talauma species. Three independent studies:
  - Morato et al. (1989): Crude extract screening identified alkaloids, saponins, phytosteroids, tannins from leaves. PMID: 2615408.
  - **Stefanello et al. (2002): Isolated 3 NEW neolignans (acetyl oleiferin-C, acetyl oleiferin-F, acetyl oleiferin-G) + 5 known neolignans (dihydroguaiaretic acid, austrobailignan-5, oleiferin-C) from bark.** This is the strongest direct evidence that Neotropical Talauma species produce neolignans. PMID: 11978427. DOI: 10.1016/s0367-326x(02)00008-4.
  - Kassuya et al. (2009): Isolated costunolide (sesquiterpene lactone) from bark with anti-inflammatory and antipyretic activity. PMID: 19524658.
- **Talauma gloriensis** (Costa Rica/Panama, sect. Talauma): Schühly et al. (2010) isolated **12 pyramidatins (dibenzocyclooctadiene-type lignans)** + Machilin G from bark. Structural characterization by X-ray crystallography and CD spectroscopy. PMID: 20663528. DOI: 10.1016/j.phytochem.2010.06.014.
- **Talauma arcabucoana** (Colombia, sect. Talauma): Barinas & Suárez (2011) isolated **aporphine alkaloids** — arcabucoine (novel), dicentrine, nordicentrine, dicentrinone — from leaves and bark. Showed cytotoxicity and antimicrobial activity against *S. aureus* and *C. albicans*. PMID: 21347974.
- **Talauma hernandezii** (Colombia): Puertas et al. (2005) found antioxidant fractions from leaf extracts comparable to alpha-tocopherol and BHA. Compounds not individually identified. PMID: 16049689.

#### Other Neotropical Magnolia (different sections)

- **Magnolia pugana** (endemic to Mexico, sect. Talauma): Essential oils from leaves, flowers, and seeds analyzed (Osorio et al. 2025); cyclocolorenone (a sesquiterpene) was the major compound (39--45% across all plant parts). No solvent extraction for lignans/neolignans was attempted. PMID: 41011669. DOI: 10.3390/molecules30183778.
- **Magnolia grandiflora** (native to southeastern USA, sect. Magnolia): Well-studied, with 76+ volatile compounds identified from flowers and bark.
- **Magnolia virginiana** (eastern USA, sect. Magnolia): Honokiol and magnolol isolated from seeds.
- **Magnolia champaca** (= *Michelia champaca*; cultivated throughout Neotropics): 99 compounds identified by GC-MS/MS. Contains alkaloids, terpenes, flavonoids, saponins. **Dengue-relevant:** Octadecadienoate derivatives from seed extract showed larvicidal and pupicidal activity against dengue vector *Aedes albopictus* (PMC 10498518). Magnolol and honokiol from *M. officinalis* also show antiparasitic activity targeting mitochondrial electron transport chain (ACS Omega, 2025).

#### Chemotaxonomic Significance of Mainland Talauma Data

The presence of neolignans in *T. ovata* (Brazil), lignans in *T. gloriensis* (Costa Rica), and aporphine alkaloids in *T. arcabucoana* (Colombia) confirms that the major Magnolia compound classes are present in sect. Talauma — the same section as the Caribbean Cubenses. This substantially strengthens the chemotaxonomic basis for the BBB database: the inference no longer relies solely on distant Asian congeners (~54 Mya divergence) but is supported by species within the same section.

**No published phytochemistry studies were found for any Caribbean endemic Magnolia species** (Cuban, Hispaniolan, Puerto Rican, or Lesser Antillean taxa).

## 4. Most Phytochemically Studied Magnolia Species

### Genus-Wide Compound Totals

- **~255 biologically active substances** isolated from different tissues of the genus Magnolia (Lee et al. 2011)
- **277 natural compounds** obtained from 11 Magnolia species in one systematic review, with 68.7% being terpenoids, phenolic compounds, and alkaloids
- The comprehensive review by Huyen et al. (2025) covering 2003--2023 in *Phytochemistry Reviews* provides the most recent genus-wide accounting
- **9 novel alkaloids + 1 nitrogen-based compound** from four Magnolia species identified in a 2002--2024 alkaloid-focused review (Borneo J. Pharmacy 2025)

### Major Compound Classes

1. **Lignans** (e.g., magnolol, honokiol, obovatol) -- the signature compounds
2. **Neolignans** (e.g., obovatol, clovanemagnolol)
3. **Alkaloids** -- mainly aporphine (liriodenine, anonaine) and benzylisoquinoline types
4. **Terpenoids** -- sesquiterpenes, monoterpenes, sesquiterpene lactones
5. **Phenylpropanoids**
6. **Essential oils**
7. **Flavonoids** (minor)
8. **Coumarins** (e.g., scopoletin)

### Key Observation for BBB Database

The phytochemical knowledge is overwhelmingly concentrated in **Asian species** used in traditional Chinese, Japanese, and Korean medicine. *M. officinalis* alone accounts for more than half of all isolated compounds. Only 2--3 New World species (*M. grandiflora*, *M. virginiana*, *M. acuminata*) have been studied phytochemically, and none of the Caribbean endemics have any published compound data.

## 5. Molecular Phylogeny of Magnolia

### Key Phylogenetic Studies

#### Azuma et al. (2001)
- **Title:** "Molecular phylogeny of the Magnoliaceae: the biogeography of tropical and temperate disjunctions"
- **Journal:** American Journal of Botany 88: 2275--2285
- **Molecular markers:** Chloroplast DNA -- matK gene + two intergenic spacers (psbA-trnH and atpB-rbcL)
- **Taxon sampling:** Broad family-level sampling; included 1--2 representatives of Caribbean Talauma/Cubenses clade
- **Key findings:** Estimated divergence times for major Magnoliaceae clades; addressed tropical-temperate biogeographic disjunctions

#### Nie et al. (2008)
- **Title:** "Phylogenetic and biogeographic complexity of Magnoliaceae in the Northern Hemisphere inferred from three nuclear data sets"
- **Journal:** Molecular Phylogenetics and Evolution 48: 1027--1040
- **Molecular markers:** Three nuclear genes -- **PHYA**, **LFY**, and **GAI1** (NOT ITS or phyB as sometimes cited)
- **Taxon sampling:** 104 samples representing 86 taxa from all sections and most subsections
- **Key findings:** 12 monophyletic groups well supported; largely consistent with sectional/subsectional classification; relaxed clock estimates place initial Magnolioideae divergence in the early Eocene (~54.57 Mya)

#### Kim & Suh (2013)
- **Title:** "Phylogeny of Magnoliaceae based on ten chloroplast DNA regions"
- **Journal:** Journal of Plant Biology 56: 290--305
- **Molecular markers:** 10 chloroplast DNA regions -- **ndhF**, **rbcL**, **matK**, **ORF350**, **trnL intron**, **trnL-trnF**, **trnH-psbA**, **rbcL-atpB**, **trnK 5' intron**, **trnK 3' intron** (8,719 bp aligned)
- **Taxon sampling:** 48 selected taxa
- **Key findings:** Major clades in molecular tree considerably differ from the currently suggested classification and traditionally recognized subgroups; deep relationships within Magnolioideae remain poorly resolved due to limited taxon sampling

#### Wang et al. (2020)
- **Title:** "Major clades and a revised classification of Magnolia and Magnoliaceae based on whole plastid genome sequences via genome skimming"
- **Journal:** Journal of Systematics and Evolution 58(5): 673--695
- **Molecular markers:** Whole plastid genomes (genome skimming approach)
- **Key findings:** 15 major clades strongly supported within Magnolia s.l., including Splendentes/Cubenses clade and Talauma clade as separate clades. Only 1--2 Caribbean representatives included.

#### Veltjen et al. (2022)
- **Title:** "The evolutionary history of the Caribbean magnolias (Magnoliaceae): Testing species delimitations and biogeographical hypotheses using molecular data"
- **Journal:** Molecular Phylogenetics and Evolution 167: 107359
- **Molecular markers:** 11 DNA regions total --
  - 5 nuclear genes: **AGT1**, **GAI1**, **LEAFY**, **PHYA**, **SQD1**
  - 3 chloroplast genes: **ndhF**, **rbcL**, **trnK**
  - 3 chloroplast intergenic spacers: **atpB-rbcL**, **ndhF-rpl32**, **psbA-trnH**
- **Taxon sampling:** **All 15 Caribbean Magnolia taxa** included, plus American mainland species and representatives of major Magnoliaceae clades
- **Key findings:** Magnolia arrived in the Caribbean in four independent events; 10 species form subsection Cubenses; *M. virginiana* subsp. *oviedoae* is in sect. Magnolia, not related to other Caribbean taxa; four taxa belong to subsect. Talauma (sister to Cubenses)

#### Dong et al. (2022)
- **Title:** "Plastid and nuclear phylogenomic incongruences and biogeographic implications of *Magnolia* s.l. (Magnoliaceae)"
- **Journal:** Journal of Systematics and Evolution. DOI: 10.1111/jse.12727
- **Key finding:** Documented significant incongruences between plastid and nuclear phylogenies for *Magnolia* s.l., complicating biogeographic inference and highlighting the importance of using both genome compartments.

#### Lozano-Contreras et al. (2024/2025)
- **Title:** "Phylogenomic insights into Neotropical Magnolia relationships"
- **Published:** Heliyon (2024)
- **Molecular markers:** Nuclear and plastid genomic data from 50 taxa (~1/3 of Neotropical species)
- **Key findings:** Within sect. Talauma, a northern subclade groups Mexican/Central American taxa and a southern subclade comprises South American + Caribbean taxa

#### Hernandez et al. (2025)
- **Title:** "There and back again: historical biogeography of neotropical magnolias based on high-throughput sequencing"
- **Journal:** BMC Ecology and Evolution. DOI: 10.1186/s12862-025-02379-7
- **Molecular markers:** High-throughput sequencing data (likely target capture or genome skimming)
- **Key findings:** Most recent phylogenomic study of Neotropical magnolias. Should be checked for inclusion of Hispaniolan species. Addresses biogeographic history including Caribbean colonization patterns.

### Are Dominican Republic Endemic Species Included?

**Yes.** The critical study is **Veltjen et al. (2022)**, which is the only published phylogeny to include **all 15 Caribbean Magnolia taxa**, including all five Hispaniola endemics (*M. domingensis*, *M. ekmanii*, *M. emarginata*, *M. hamorii*, *M. pallescens*). Earlier family-wide studies (Azuma 2001, Kim & Suh 2013, Wang et al. 2020) included only 1--2 Caribbean representatives, none specifically from the Dominican Republic.

Claerhout et al. (2023) additionally used 16 microsatellite markers for population genetics of the three Dominican species but this was a conservation genetics study, not a phylogeny per se.

## Summary of Key Findings for BBB Database

1. **Taxonomic placement is settled:** Dominican Magnolias belong to Magnolia sect. Talauma subsect. Cubenses within a monophyletic Caribbean clade.

2. **Phytochemical knowledge gap is absolute:** Zero published phytochemistry studies exist for any Caribbean endemic Magnolia species. The chemotaxonomic transfer approach (inferring likely compounds from phylogenetically related, well-studied species) is the only viable strategy.

3. **Closest well-studied relatives:** The Asian species in sect. Talauma and the broader Magnolia genus (especially *M. officinalis* with >200 compounds, *M. obovata*, *M. grandiflora*) provide the chemotaxonomic reference pool. However, the phylogenetic distance between Asian and Caribbean clades is substantial (divergence ~54 Mya).

4. **Molecular data confirmed in GenBank:** 78 nucleotide sequences across 7 voucher specimens, 11 DNA regions per species, plus 2 complete chloroplast genomes for *M. domingensis* (OR730687, OR730688; ~160 kbp each). Veltjen et al. (2022) provide the most comprehensive molecular dataset for Caribbean Magnolias, enabling phylogenetic distance calculations that could weight chemotaxonomic predictions.

5. **Conservation imperative:** All five Hispaniola endemics are Endangered or Critically Endangered, making destructive chemical sampling ethically constrained. In silico and chemoinformatic approaches are not just scientifically convenient but may be the only ethical option for compound prediction.
