## 3. Implications for BBB Database

1. **Phylogenetic placement is complete.** All three DR species have 11-region molecular phylogenies placing them within the Caribbean clade of Magnolia sect. Talauma. Phylogenetic distances to well-studied Asian congeners can be computed.

2. **Complete chloroplast genomes exist for M. domingensis.** This is the most endangered of the three. The ~160 kbp genomes (OR730687, OR730688) enable plastid phylogenomics.

3. **No phytochemistry data exist.** Zero published chemical profiles for any of the three species. The BBB database therefore relies entirely on chemotaxonomic inference from the genus-wide compound pool.

4. **GBIF occurrence data provide geographic context** for potential ecological/phytochemical correlations but are not directly used in the database build.
